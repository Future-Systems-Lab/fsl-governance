## Editorial Suitability

This manuscript presents the first system, to our knowledge, to unify authentication, informed consent, session authorization, and behavioral health data attribution into a single EIP-191 cryptographic wallet signature event. The work advances the blockchain-in-healthcare sector by demonstrating that behavioral health engagement data — the most stigma-sensitive category of health information — can be captured on a public blockchain with full participant sovereignty and zero personally identifiable information. The proof-of-concept deployment on Ethereum Sepolia, with nine verified smart contracts and a comparative evaluation against four established systems, provides an immediately reproducible architectural reference for the BHTY readership.
