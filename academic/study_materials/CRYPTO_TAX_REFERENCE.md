# Crypto Tax Reference — FSL Sovereign Guides & Participants

> **Disclaimer:** This document is educational reference material, not tax advice. Consult a CPA or tax professional familiar with cryptocurrency before making tax decisions.

---

## Crypto as Property (US Tax Law)

Per **IRS Notice 2014-21** and subsequent guidance, cryptocurrency is treated as **property** — not currency — for US federal tax purposes. This means every crypto transaction is potentially a taxable event.

## Income Recognition

**When you receive crypto (HNT, ETH, EHT, etc.):**
- Taxed as **ordinary income** at **fair market value (FMV) on the date received**
- Applies to: session fees, HNT rewards, BenevolenceFund distributions, SovereignAchievement credentials with monetary value
- Your **cost basis** = FMV at time of receipt
- Report on Schedule C (self-employment) if received as independent practitioner income

**Example:** You receive 100 HNT when HNT is worth $0.50 each → $50 ordinary income reported on Schedule C.

## Capital Gains / Losses

**When you sell, swap, or spend crypto:**
- **Short-term capital gain** (held <1 year): taxed at ordinary income rate
- **Long-term capital gain** (held >1 year): taxed at 0%, 15%, or 20% depending on income bracket
- **Loss:** deductible against capital gains, up to $3,000/year against ordinary income
- **Cost basis:** FMV at time of original receipt (see above)

**Example:** You received 100 HNT at $0.50 each ($50 basis). Later sell at $1.00 each ($100 proceeds). Capital gain = $50.

## Self-Employment Tax (Sovereign Guides)

As an independent practitioner:
- Session income is **self-employment income**
- Subject to **15.3% SE tax** (12.4% Social Security + 2.9% Medicare) on net earnings
- Report on **Schedule SE** in addition to Schedule C
- Deductible: 50% of SE tax is an above-the-line deduction

## Quarterly Estimated Payments

If you expect to owe >$1,000 in taxes:
- File **Form 1040-ES** quarterly
- Due dates: April 15, June 15, September 15, January 15
- Underpayment penalty if you don't pay enough quarterly

## Cost Basis Tracking

You must track:
- Date of each crypto receipt
- FMV at time of receipt (your cost basis)
- Date of each disposal (sale, swap, spend)
- FMV at time of disposal (your proceeds)
- Method: FIFO, LIFO, or specific identification (choose one, be consistent)

## State-by-State Variation

- **No state income tax:** AK, FL, NV, NH (limited), SD, TN (limited), TX, WA, WY
- **Crypto-friendly states:** WY (DAO LLC framework), TX, FL
- **High-tax states with crypto enforcement:** CA, NY, NJ
- **NC (Dr. Meg's state):** Standard state income tax applies to crypto income; no special crypto provisions

## FSL-Specific Notes

- HNT is an ERC-20 token on Ethereum Sepolia (testnet) — **testnet tokens have $0 FMV** and are not taxable
- When FSL migrates to mainnet, tokens will have real market value and all income recognition rules apply
- BenevolenceFund distributions (annual April 1) are taxable at FMV on distribution date
- SovereignAchievement credentials are soulbound (non-transferable) — taxability depends on whether they have market value at issuance
- Session payments via USDC are taxable at face value ($1 per USDC)
