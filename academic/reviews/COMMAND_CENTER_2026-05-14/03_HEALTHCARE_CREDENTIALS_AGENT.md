# Healthcare/Credentials Agent -- FSL Command Center Review

**Date:** 2026-05-14
**Surface:** fsl-command-center.vercel.app (index.html)
**Perspective:** Healthcare professional, behavioral health credentialing body, or clinical reviewer

---

## WHAT'S GOOD

1. **Credentials are displayed in the footer.** "Dr. Meg Montanez-Davenport, D.N.Psy., BCHN, CBHP" -- all three credential abbreviations are present. D.N.Psy. (Doctor of Natural Psychotherapy), BCHN (Board Certified in Holistic Nutrition), and CBHP (Certified Behavioral Health Professional) are listed.

2. **NPI number is public and verifiable.** NPI 1497696264 with taxonomy code 175F00000X is listed in the Public Legitimacy Indicators section. Anyone can verify this on the NPPES NPI Registry.

3. **Non-clinical positioning is clear.** The disclaimer explicitly states: "This platform is an educational technology demonstration. It does not provide medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment." The zero-PHI statement reinforces this.

4. **"Sovereign Guide" model is named.** The sprint history Phase 1 list includes "Sovereign Guide model" and the Phase 4 roadmap mentions "International Sovereign Guide network." The thesis section references "Sovereign Guide framework" in the Therapeutic layer.

5. **AlchemistForge shadow work is framed as case study.** "Dr. Meg is Case Study Participant #1. A shadow aspect has been recorded on-chain" -- this positions it as research, not clinical practice.

6. **The L1/L2/L3 therapeutic framework maps to specific neurotransmitter systems.** L1 = GABA (hypnosis), L2 = Serotonin (orthomolecular), L3 = Endocannabinoid (inner child). This shows clinical knowledge applied to game design.

---

## WHAT'S MISSING

1. **Credentials are ONLY in the footer.** A healthcare reviewer looking for Dr. Meg's qualifications must scroll to the very bottom of the page. The credentials are not in the hero, not in the academic reviewer note, and not in any dedicated "About the Founder" section.

2. **No professional affiliation listing.** ISOM (International Society for Orthomolecular Medicine), NANP (National Association of Nutrition Professionals), or any other professional memberships are not mentioned anywhere on the page.

3. **The "Sovereign Guide" model is named but never explained.** What is a Sovereign Guide? How does it differ from a therapist, counselor, or coach? What scope of practice does it operate within? A healthcare reviewer or credentialing body needs this defined.

4. **No credential verification links.** The NPI is listed but not hyperlinked to the NPPES lookup. The D.N.Psy., BCHN, and CBHP credentials have no links to issuing bodies or verification portals.

5. **The educational/non-clinical boundary is understated.** While the disclaimer says "does not provide medical advice," the game descriptions use clinical language ("trance-state induction," "somatic memory integration," "safety-boundary mechanics") that could raise flags with a clinical reviewer. The boundary between educational technology and clinical intervention needs stronger demarcation.

6. **No clinical experience timeline or practice history.** The CV is linked but the page itself does not mention Dr. Meg's years of practice, areas of specialization, or clinical background. A healthcare reviewer wants to see this at a glance.

7. **AlchemistForge "Jungian shadow work" framing may concern clinical psychologists.** The contract description says "Jungian shadow work -- Identify/Transmute/Celebrate." Jungian analysis is a specific psychotherapeutic modality. The on-chain framing should clarify whether this is therapeutic intervention or personal development/educational tool.

8. **No informed consent language for game participants.** The games involve "trance-state induction" (L1) and "somatic memory integration" (L3). Where is the participant informed consent model? The ToS is mentioned for the platform but not for individual game sessions.
