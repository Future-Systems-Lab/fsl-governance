## DOCUMENT 1: Letter of Intent (LOI_ASU_DEng_text.md)

### VERDICT: MINOR FIXES

### CRITICAL

1. **Game count claim is inflated.** The LOI states "45 browser-based therapeutic games (15 per level across L1 Hypnosis, L2 Orthomolecular, and L3 Inner Child)." The CV correctly describes "three flagship demos (one per level)" with architecture that "supports expansion to full catalog." The LOI must be corrected to state 3 demos, not 45. Claiming 45 deployed games when 3 exist is a material misrepresentation that could disqualify the application if reviewed against the live platform.

2. **Date header reads "January 2027" for a Spring 2027 application with an October 1, 2026 priority deadline.** If submitting before the priority deadline, the letter date should reflect the actual submission date (likely September/October 2026). January 2027 suggests submission after the priority deadline and possibly after the regular deadline. Verify intended submission timing and correct.

### HIGH

3. **SunCodes date range inconsistency.** The LOI states "SunCodes Holistic Health (2018-2023)." The CV states "2018 - 2024." These must match across documents. Pick one and make both consistent.

In 2019, when pandemic-driven hygienist shortages..." — but the pandemic began in early 2020, not 2019. The CV lists Riccobene as "2019 - 2025." If the C-suite recruitment happened in response to pandemic-driven shortages, the timeline should read 2020, not 2019. Alternatively, if 2019 is correct, drop "pandemic-driven" from the LOI framing since the pandemic had not yet started. This is a factual precision issue an admissions reviewer could flag.

5. **BS MIS is listed as "Conferred July 2026" on the CV.** The LOI should explicitly acknowledge this degree will be conferred before the Spring 2027 start. Currently the LOI's "Basic Qualifications" section lists it without the conferral date, which could read as already completed. Since today is May 2026 and conferral is July 2026, this is nearly resolved — but the LOI should not present it ambiguously.

### MEDIUM

6. **"Outside HIPAA regulatory scope" framing is correct but needs consistent phrasing.** The LOI mostly gets this right — "FSL itself holds no Protected Health Information by architectural design" — but the first research question drifts into "regulatory-grade privacy standards" and "HIPAA-regulated behavioral health as the highest-bar test case" in the same sentence where it clarifies FSL holds no PHI. This is good but could confuse a reviewer unfamiliar with the distinction. Recommend a single, crisp canonical statement early (e.g., "FSL operates outside HIPAA scope by architectural design — it holds no PHI") and reference it consistently rather than re-explaining it each time.

7. **Patent reference is clean.** U.S. Provisional Patent Application No. 64/063,037, filed 11 May 2026, is referenced in both documents consistently. No issue here.

8. **Narrative length and tone.** The personal narrative is powerful but occupies roughly 40% of the LOI. For an engineering doctorate application, the admissions committee will want to see the technical proposal and faculty alignment given at least equal weight. Consider whether the narrative section (paragraphs 1-6) can be compressed by 20-30% without losing emotional force, giving more space to the applied project section.

9. **"Sovereign Guides" vs. "Providers" terminology.** The LOI uses "Sovereign Guide" in research question 2 ("Sovereign Guide participation"). Confirm this aligns with the current platform terminology. The CV references "provider credentialing system" and "Provider Portal" in several places. If the platform has adopted "Sovereign Guides" as the user-facing term, the CV should reflect this in descriptive text (routes and database names can remain unchanged).

### LOW

11. **"I believe the universe placed me in charge of building it" — tone calibration.** This sentence may read differently to an engineering admissions committee than to a clinical audience. Not necessarily a problem, but worth a conscious decision about whether to keep it.

12. **AI Use Disclosure section is present but empty.** Line 61 says "Listing 1: AI Use Disclosure" with no content. Either populate it or remove the placeholder.

- **Faculty alignment is exceptionally well-researched.** Boscovic (blockchain infrastructure), Ahn/SEFCOM (identity/access control), and Ghasemzadeh (digital health/behavioral informatics) form a coherent triad. The LOI should be precise about what "live" and "deployed" mean in context — testnet is not mainnet, and the distinction matters to blockchain-literate reviewers like Boscovic.
